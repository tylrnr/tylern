Close Stance in the Deadlift

Tyler N | January 2024

The close stance in deadlifts is effective due to an outward knee placement, shortening the femur's anteroposterior component for a hip position closer to the bar...

Knees out externally rotates the femurs, allowing the adductors to participate in the pull as hip extensors. This stance benefits lifters with long femurs for a better starting position...

In the Clean/Snatch

Knees out in the clean/snatch aims for a more efficient back angle, clearing the knees from the bar path. A wide grip provides space for knees to be pointed out from a wider stance...

In the 1960s, Japanese weightlifters introduced the "frog leg style" with heels nearly touching and knees/toes flared out. While effective for some Olympians, it was inefficient for most people...

Start Position

1. Take a narrow stance with shins one inch from the bar, toes pointed somewhat out.
2. Bend at the waist, take your usual deadlift grip for efficiency.
3. Bend knees in line with toes to touch shins to the bar.
4. Take a big breath, lift your chest to rotate up between your arms.
5. Push the floor away and drag the bar up your legs.
